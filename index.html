Mt Giluwe

Mount Giluwe is PNG's second-highest mountain at 4367 m (or 14327 feet). It is the highest volcanic peak in Oceania. Mt Giluwe is located in the Southern Highlands province and is an old shield volcano with vast alpine grasslands. Ancient volcanic plugs form its two summits, with the central peak the highest and an east peak about 2 kilometers (1.2 mi) away at 4,300 m (14,108 ft).


Background Information

Mt Giluwe's flanks are covered by many lava flows, which reach up to 30 m thickness. The flanks are cut by radial erosion valley which provide excellent outcrops of volcanic strata. There are many flank cinders cones with up to 30-meter-deep craters and up to 200 m diameter. Most of the flank craters are breached to the downward side. The large shield of Mt Giluwe has a basal diameter of 30 km. It receives an annual rainfall of 2500 mm and frost can occur above 4000 m. Most of the mountain is covered with montane rain forest and grassland. Subglacial eruptions of Mt Giluwe volcano: During the Pleistocene, Mt Giluwe was covered by an ice cap which extended as low as the 3200 to 3500 m level. The volcano erupted under the ice during its last period of activity. Subglacial eruptions from volcanoes in the tropics are elsewhere only known from Kilimanjaro and Mauna Kea.


Why you would like to come explore Mount Giluwe?

Mt Giluwe is the highest volcano in Pacific Oceania and considered one of "the 7 volcanic summits" (not Mauna Kea on Hawaii, as many thinks: Giluwe is 162 m higher). It is an ancient extinct shield volcano with 2 prominent 400 m high cones forming its summit, called the main peak and the slightly lower eastern peak (4300 m). Occasionally, the summit receives snow fall.

Safty Guide

Some important trekking tips/guides.

Carry your backpack

Carring your bagpack is part of your trekking experience. Your backpack can be carried by you or a local porter; However, make sure to pack light so you wont feel the burden of the weight when trekking. Some essential things to include in your bagpack are medication, toiletries, food, torch, and water bottles,

Wear a Jacket

Wearing a Jacket protect you from the percipetation, wind, and cold through out your trekking journey. The weather at Mt Giluwe can be unprediatable hence, Wearing a jacket can keep you warm during your journey. During the day when the weather is fine, the temperature can be between 10°C-12°C and in the mornings and in the evenings the min temperature is 3°C and the maximun temperature is 5°C.

Wear Trekking shoe

Although trekking shoes are heavier then regular shoes it's a must that you wear them so you can protect your feet against bruising, abrasion from rough wilderness terrain and avoid spraining your ankle.

Bring food

Eating food before trekking makes you feel energizetics for a longer time; However, bring food and snack so you can have them along the way. If you book the local trekking package, then you will have to provide your own food but if you book the trekking package with the lodge then you can contact them to find out if they will provide food or not.

Bring your tent

Mt Giluwe is less then two day climb. It takes about 4 hours for seasoned mountain trekkers and 5 hours for the amateur or novice from the base camp to the summit of Mt Giluwe, hence for those who wants to camp, bring your own tent. Mt Giluwe is really cold in the night, hence a tent will protect you from the cold weather but make sure to bring a lightweight model so you don't feel the burden of the weight. It's okay if you don't want to bring a tent because there's a cave at the top of Mt Giluwe where you can take shelter.

other essential things

Bring along other essential things you might need when treeking Mt Giluwe, things like; toiletries, lighter for starting a fire, water for keeping you hydrated and many other things but make sure you're not overpacked.

Travel route

If you are a tourist who wants to trek Mt Giluwe, the second largest mountain in Papua New Guinea (PNG). If you are comming in from other countries, first you'll arrive at Jackson's International Airport which is in Port Morsby, the capital of PNG. After that you'll travel to Mt Hagen, Kagamuga Airport which is the nearest Airport from Mt Giluwe. If you are looking for a place to stay, contact the nearest lodges in Mt Hagen and in Southern Highlands.
